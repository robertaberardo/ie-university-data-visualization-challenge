UNDERSTANDING THE SEASONALITY OF THE BOOKINGS
Objective: HotelFlix aims to better understand when their customers make their reservations and increase the revenue creating a price model that increases the price of reservations in high season and decreases in low season.

The weekly booking trends in Portugal show consistent reservations with peaks around weeks 20 and 42, likely due to summer holidays and early fall. High Demand Periods: May (Weeks 19-21) and October (Weeks 40-42)

The data indicates significant peaks in bookings around late May and mid-October, aligning with weeks 20 and 42. During these periods, the demand for reservations is at its highest, reflecting popular travel times such as spring and fall holidays. To maximize revenue, it would be strategic to implement higher pricing plans during these months. Special offers and packages tailored for these peak seasons can attract more bookings while justifying the increased rates.
Low Demand Periods: January to March (Weeks 1-12)

The beginning of the year, spanning January through March, shows lower booking levels across all regions. This period typically experiences reduced travel activity post-holiday season and before the onset of spring. To stimulate demand during these months, introducing lower pricing plans, promotional deals, and targeted marketing campaigns can attract more customers. Emphasizing off-peak travel benefits, such as quieter tourist attractions and discounted rates, may encourage bookings during this slower season.

0 - 7 DAYs: 90% Penalty

Bookings canceled within 0 to 7 days of the arrival date should incur a 90% penalty. This high penalty is necessary because the short notice leaves minimal time to refill the reservation, leading to a significant loss in potential revenue.
8 - 45 DAYs: 60% Penalty

For bookings canceled between 8 and 45 days before arrival, a 60% penalty should be imposed. While there is more time to potentially rebook the room compared to the 0-7 day window, the loss in revenue remains substantial.
46 - 90 DAYs: 25% Penalty

Cancellations made 46 to 90 days before arrival should be subject to a 25% penalty. The increased lead time allows for a better chance to rebook the room, thus minimizing the revenue loss.
FROM 91 DAYs: No penalty

For cancellations made more than 91 days before the arrival date, no penalty should be applied. The extended lead time provides ample opportunity to rebook the room, mitigating the impact of the cancellation.
Previous Cancellations

There is an opportunity to impose penalties for recurring cancellations, as 13% of cancellations are made by clients who have previously canceled.
PREPARING THE IT SYSTEMS FOR THE DEMAND
Objective: HotelFlix wants to know when they will have peak demands in their booking system to provide a consistent solution without any interruption based on the past booking data that they have.
The chart illustrates the correlation between reservation months and arrival months, providing crucial insights for HotelFlix to anticipate peak demands in their booking system. Observing the data, we see significant booking activities between January 2016 and August 2017, with notable peaks in May and October. The right-side histogram shows that the majority of bookings are made within the first 50 days before the arrival date, indicating a high volume of short-term reservations.

To ensure a consistent booking experience without interruptions, it is essential to prepare the IT infrastructure for these peak periods. The data suggests the need for enhanced system capacity and robust performance management around May and October when the highest booking activities occur. By aligning IT resources and capabilities with these demand patterns, HotelFlix can optimize their system's performance, ensuring seamless service during both peak and off-peak times.
